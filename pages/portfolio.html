Artist Statement

The photograph isn’t made. It’s waited for.

Randy Allen  ·  The Infinite Arch  ·  Fine Art Landscape Photography

“The most honest photographs aren’t taken — they’re waited for. They come to the photographer who arrives early, stays late, and returns when the light changes.”

The Infinite Arch takes its name from two things at once: the natural arch in rock and the arch of a human life. Both describe the same passage — a threshold between what was and what’s possible.

I shoot with Fujifilm’s GFX medium format system because 102 megapixels demands intention. Every frame is a choice. Every trip into the field is a commitment to be present for whatever the light decides to do.

This work is landscape as contemplation. The camera is the last step in a process that begins with arriving, waiting, and being willing to return. The image is the record of that patience.

— Beyond the Daydream
