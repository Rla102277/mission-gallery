the Infinite ARCH
Beyond the Daydream

Artist Statement  ·  Fine Art Landscape Photography

Most of us spend our lives just outside the frame.

We scroll past the image, save it for later, and move on. The daydream is the beautiful moment we never quite inhabit.

✶

I started chasing the places that couldn't be scrolled past.

The Infinite Arch is built on a belief that the most honest photographs aren't taken — they're waited for. They come to the photographer who arrives early, stays late, and returns when the light changes. Who understands that the arch in the rock and the arch of a life are describing the same passage: a threshold between what was and what's possible.

I shoot with Fujifilm's GFX medium format system because the format demands presence. You can't spray and pray at 102 megapixels. You choose your moment, and you mean it. My kit is the GFX 100RF, the GFX 50R, the X-E5, and a Konica Hexar — each one chosen for what it sees, not what it proves.

This is photography as contemplation. Landscape as mirror. Light as metaphor.

Sean O'Connell didn't say number 25 is technically perfect or compositionally superior. He said it was the quintessence of life. The difference matters to me. He was talking about the photograph that captured something true — not flawless, but undeniably alive. That's the standard I'm chasing. Not the image that impresses, but the one that's irreplaceable. The one I had to be cold for, had to wait for, had to raise the camera toward the impossible light and believe something was there before I could see it. This is what I came for. This is the quintessence of life.

Welcome to the other side of the daydream.

“Beautiful things don’t ask for attention.”

— Sean O’Connell
